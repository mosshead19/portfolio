Adobong Sitaw

Adobong Sitaw, also known as String Bean Adobo, is a popular Filipino dish. It combines tender string beans with a savory and tangy sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and onion. Sometimes pork belly is added for extra flavor and richness. This dish is typically simmered until the beans are tender and the sauce is slightly reduced, making it a perfect accompaniment to steamed rice. üçõ

Ingredients

  • 2 ties sitaw/string beans
  • garlic
  • onion
  • black pepper
  • oyster sauce
  • 2 cups water

Steps

  1. Pour 2 tbsp oil into the pan.
  2. Add garlic and onion, cook until fragrant.
  3. Put in chopped string beans.
  4. Add water to the level of the string beans, boil to soften.
  5. When almost dry, pour in oyster sauce.
  6. wait for few minutes to lower the temp then serve
